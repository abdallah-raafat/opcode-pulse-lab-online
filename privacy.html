Privacy policy

Last updated July 15, 2026

Information this service stores

When you create an account, Firebase Authentication stores your email, sign-in provider, verification state, display name, and account identifier. Your completed profile may include country, role, school, or organization.

Depending on your plan, the service may store saved projects, compile history, diagnostic summaries, support messages, login-session statistics, and public network risk information. IP-based network location is an estimate, not a precise device location.

The site requests browser-provided device coordinates only after you press Share this login location and approve the browser permission prompt. If you choose to share, it stores latitude, longitude, the browser-reported accuracy radius, and capture time for that login. Only the permanent owner administrator can read these precise-location records. Denying or dismissing the request does not prevent access and stores no coordinates. The service does not collect your Google password.

Google sign-in

Google sign-in is used only for basic identity authentication. The application requests the standard name, email address, and profile photo made available by Firebase Authentication. It does not request Gmail, Drive, contacts, calendar, or other sensitive Google data.

How information is used

Account data keeps projects linked to the correct verified user, applies subscription access, supports troubleshooting, prevents blocked-account access, and provides private administrator statistics. Browser-provided coordinates are displayed as user-approved device information with an accuracy radius; they are not treated as verified identity or guaranteed proof of country. Support messages are visible to authorized administrators so the owner can reply manually.

Control and deletion

You can decline location sharing without losing access. You can stop using the service and request account, project, network, or precise-location deletion by contacting the owner. Permanent account deletion also removes stored coordinates. Invitation codes record the account that redeemed them so usage limits can be enforced.
